Zoom — Business Model Canvas (Simple English)

Short and clear slides about Zoom's business model. Images and simple English.

Key Partnerships

Zoom works with partners to keep service reliable and fast.

  • Cloud providers (data centers)
  • Hardware makers (cameras, microphones)
  • Integration partners (Slack, Microsoft)
  • Security & compliance vendors

Key Activities

Main things Zoom does every day.

  • Run and improve video conferencing platform
  • Keep servers and networks fast
  • Update apps and add features
  • Support customers and security

Key Resources

What Zoom needs to run the product.

  • Global server network (cloud)
  • Skilled engineers and support team
  • Software code and patents
  • Brand and large user base

Value Propositions

Simple reasons customers choose Zoom.

  • Easy to use video calls and meetings
  • Works on many devices (phone, PC, tablet)
  • Good quality even on slow internet
  • Free basic plan and paid plans for businesses
  • Integrations with other work tools

Customer Relationships

How Zoom connects with users.

  • Self-service (easy sign up)
  • Online help and tutorials
  • Business support plans
  • Community and developer resources

Channels

Ways users get Zoom.

  • Zoom website (download apps)
  • App stores (iOS, Android)
  • Business sales & resellers
  • Integrations inside other apps

Customer Segments

Who uses Zoom?

  • Individual users (friends, families)
  • Small businesses and teams
  • Large enterprises and schools
  • Developers and integrators

Cost Structure

Main costs for Zoom to run the service.

  • Server and cloud costs
  • Engineering and product teams
  • Customer support and sales
  • Marketing and security/compliance

Revenue Streams

How Zoom makes money.

  • Paid subscriptions (Pro, Business, Enterprise)
  • Zoom Phone and add-on services
  • Hardware sales and certified partners
  • Marketplace integrations and platform fees
